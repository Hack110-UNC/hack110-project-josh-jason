Downfalls

50% 30% 20%

When accounting your subscriptions, make sure to allocate 50% for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for debts/saving. Make sure your subscriptions do not exceed these catagories. Needs include grocieries, transportation, utilities, rent/mortgage, and insurance.

Unused Subscriptions

Make sure that all the subscriptions that ones you use. If the show is done or a season of sports is done, cancel it. Furthermore, always reasearch for altenatives could help you to save. There are always companies trying to out do others, so new competitors might make cheaper alternatives.

Impulsive spending

In a world full of marketing, people often spend based on their emoptions since marketing aims to evoke certain emotions. As a result, think about the viability of the product and how often you will use it. Furthermore, double check the return policy just in case you can return it.

Unnecessary Convience

Sometimes convince may double the costs without you knowing it. They might add a little bit more to the porduct, then add a convince fee either through delivery or other methods. Evealuate if the opportunity cost cancels out the need for convience. You get other benefits besides economics such as health and the opportunity to interact with others.

Forgetfulness

Make sure to use our website to double check if you are missing anything. Sometimes people forget about subscriptions so if your output doesn't align with your statements, there might be something missing. Statements is a good place to check what you are missing.
