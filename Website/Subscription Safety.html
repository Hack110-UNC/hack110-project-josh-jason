Subscription Safety Tips

50/30/20 Rule

When accounting for your subscriptions, allocate 50% for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for debts/savings. Needs include groceries, transportation, utilities, rent/mortgage, and insurance. Ensure your subscriptions do not exceed these categories.

Unused Subscriptions

Cancel subscriptions you no longer use. For example, if a show or sports season is over, cancel the subscription. Research alternatives to save money, as new competitors often offer cheaper options.

Impulsive Spending

Marketing often targets emotions, leading to impulsive spending. Evaluate the product's viability and how often you'll use it. Check the return policy in case you need to return it.

Unnecessary Convenience

Convenience can double costs without you realizing it. Evaluate whether the opportunity cost outweighs the need for convenience. Consider other benefits, such as health and social interaction.

Forgetfulness

Use our website to double-check your subscriptions. Sometimes people forget about subscriptions. If your output doesn't align with your statements, there might be something missing. Statements are a good place to check for forgotten subscriptions.
